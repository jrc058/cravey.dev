      Scones

      Recipes

      üçΩ Scones ->#

      üçú Ingredients#

      1. 1.5 cups (240g) 1:1 gluten free flour
      2. 0.25 cups (50g) sugar
        1. for savory scones, 1 tablespoon
      3. 1 tablespoon baking powder
      4. 0.5 teaspoon salt
      5. 8 tablespoon (13g) frozen butter
      6. 0.33-0.5 cup of mix-ins
      7. 1 egg
      8. 1 teaspoon vanilla
        1. for savory scones, none
      9. 0.66 cup sour cream (yogurt?)

      üìë Directions#

      1. Combine dry and sugar
      2. Grate butter and cut in
      3. Add mix-ins
      4. Separate bowl, combine wet
      5. Stir in wet until combined
      6. Put dough out and wrap in plastic wrap
      7. Put in fridge for 20-30min
      8. Freeze for 15min
      9. Preheat over to 500F
      10. Cut dough
      11. Put in oven and lower temp to 425F
      12. Bake for 12-14min

      Snowball Cookies

      Recipes

      üçΩ Snowball Cookies ->#

      üçú Ingredients#

      1. 1 cup butter
      2. 0.5 cup powdered sufar
      3. 1 teaspoon vanilla exxtract
      4. 2.25 cups flour
      5. 0.75 cup pecans, finely chopped
      6. 0.5 teaspoon salt
      7. Powder sugar for rolling

      üìë Directions#

      1. Preheat oven to 400F
      2. In large bowl, cream:
        1. Butter
        2. Vanilla
        3. Powdered sugar
      3. Add and combine until holds together:
        1. Flour
        2. Salt
        3. Nuts
      4. Shape into 1 inch balls. Do not make too big
      5. Space 1 inch apart on parchment lined cookie sheet
      6. Freeze for 5 minutes
      7. Bake for 10-12mins, should NOT be brown
      8. Allow to cool for 3-5mins
      9. Roll in powdered sugar

      2. 1 cup sugar

      üìë Directions#

      1. In pre-heated medium hot saucepan:
        1. Whisk together
        2. Let sugar dissolve

      Vanilla Custard

      Recipes

      üçΩ Vanilla Custard ->#

      It’s a favorite. Costco ice cream is close, but this homemade custard is the best sweet treat in the world. It’s a tedious process and would probably be improved by getting the Kitchen-Aid specific bowl attachment, but right now we have the Kitchen-Aid countertop ice cream spinner thing that works.

      üçú Ingredients#

      1. 4 cups whole milk
        1. can sub half and half or cream
      2. 1.25 cups sugar
      3. 0.33 cup corn starch
      4. 0.125 teaspoon salt
      5. 4 eggs
      6. 2 tablespoon vanilla extract
        1. The nicer the better
      7. 14 ounce sweetened condensed milk

      üìë Directions#

      1. In large saucepan, whisk:
        1. Corn starch
        2. Milk
        3. Sugar
      2. Heat mixture to 175F, while whisking constantly
      3. In small bowl:
        1. Whisk 2 eggs
        2. Acclimate eggs by ladling hot mixture from large saucepan slowly and gently into eggs, so they don’t curdle.
        3. Once eggs are properly acclimated to heat, pour eggs into large saucepan
      4. Heat large saucepan on low, continuing to whisk constantly.
      5. Mixture should reach at least 160F, coating the back of a spoon
      6. Remove from heat
      7. Immediately place in ice bath and stir for 2 minutes
      8. Stir in sweetened condensed milk and vanilla
      9. Put wax paper on surface of mixture, put in fridge overnight
      10. Using ice cream maker:
        1. Pour mixture into frozen cylinder, two-thirds full
        2. Fridge the rest of mixture
        3. Let mix spin until thickened (approx 30 minutes, depending)
      11. Freeze ice cream in tupperware for 2-4 hours minimum before serving
